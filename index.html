Amsterdam

ISMRM Diffusion workshop

October 10-14, 2022 Quentin Uhl

I had the opportunity to attend my second conference as a PhD student in Amsterdam, and it was a very different experience from my first one. While the first conference was much larger and broader in scope, this one was much smaller and focused specifically on diffusion MRI. This made for a much more intense conference experience, as every talk had a direct connection to our research and there was no downtime like there was at the first conference.

Despite the intensity, I was thrilled to be selected to present one of my two selected abstracts in the Proffered Oral session. It was a great opportunity to share my work and get feedback from my peers. Overall, the conference was extremely productive and I came away with a lot of new ideas for my future research.

London

ISMRM 2022 & post-ISMRM workshop : Microstructure Imaging meets Machine Learning

May 9-13, 2022 Quentin Uhl

I had the opportunity to attend my first conference as a PhD student in London, and it was an incredible experience. After 7 months of research, I was thrilled to have the opportunity to be a part of this huge gathering of MRI researchers. The conference was even better than I imagined, with thousands of attendees filling the massive exhibition halls for talks and presentations.

One of the highlights of the conference for me was the post-conference meeting at the UCL Centre for Medical Imaging Computing. It was here that I really had the chance to connect with other researchers in my specific field of diffusion MRI and machine learning. It was such a great feeling to meet and learn from others in my specific field. Overall, the conference was an unforgettable experience, filled with great memories and valuable connections. I am really grateful to have been a part of it.
